Why Move to Herriman Utah: The Complete 2026 Guide

10 min read • Updated February 2026

Herriman, Utah has become one of the most talked-about cities in the American West. Once a quiet farming community, it's now Utah's fastest-growing city, attracting families, professionals, and retirees who want mountain views, excellent schools, and a quality of life that's hard to find anywhere else.

If you're considering a move to Herriman — whether from another state or elsewhere in Utah — this comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about living in this remarkable community.

Location & Geography

Herriman sits in southwest Salt Lake County, nestled against the Oquirrh Mountains with panoramic views of the Wasatch Range to the east. This elevated position — especially in communities like Panorama — offers residents daily views of Utah's most iconic mountains.

The city is approximately 25 miles from downtown Salt Lake City, with easy access via Mountain View Corridor, Bangerter Highway, and I-15. Salt Lake City International Airport is about 30 minutes away.

Why Families Choose Herriman

Excellent Schools

Herriman is served by Jordan School District, consistently rated among Utah's best. The district features:

  • Multiple A-rated elementary schools (Blackridge, Butterfield Canyon)
  • Top-performing middle schools (Fort Herriman, Copper Mountain)
  • Award-winning high schools (Herriman High, Mountain Ridge High)
  • Strong STEM and arts programs
  • Active sports and extracurricular activities

Safety & Community

Herriman consistently ranks as one of Utah's safest cities. The community maintains a small-town feel despite its growth, with annual festivals, farmers markets, and neighborhood events that foster genuine connections between residents.

Outdoor Recreation

Living in Herriman means living an outdoor lifestyle:

  • Butterfield Canyon: Hiking, mountain biking, and horseback riding
  • Rose Canyon: Trail running and wildlife viewing
  • Skiing: Brighton, Solitude, Snowbird, and Alta are 45 minutes away
  • Community Parks: Over 30 parks and open spaces

The Panorama Advantage

As Herriman's last foothills development, Panorama offers the most dramatic Wasatch views in the city — plus world-class amenities like the three-level Panorama Park, miles of trails, and premium builders creating homes from the $400s to $800s+.

Cost of Living

While Herriman isn't the cheapest Utah city, it offers exceptional value compared to similar communities in Denver, Phoenix, or California. Key factors:

  • Housing: New construction from the low $400s (at Panorama); median home price around $550,000
  • Property Taxes: Utah's property taxes are relatively low
  • Utilities: Below national average
  • No State Income Tax on Social Security: Great for retirees

Employment & Economy

Herriman residents enjoy access to Utah's booming economy:

  • Tech Hub: Silicon Slopes companies are 15-30 minutes away
  • Healthcare: Major hospital systems nearby
  • Remote Work: Many residents work remotely, enjoying Herriman's quality of life
  • Salt Lake City: Full urban job market within 30-minute commute

Shopping & Dining

Mountain View Village, Herriman's retail hub, offers:

  • Harmon's Grocery
  • Cinemark movie theater
  • Dozens of restaurants
  • Retail and service shops

Additional shopping at nearby South Jordan (The District) and Riverton provides even more options.

Climate

Herriman enjoys a four-season climate:

  • Summer: Warm and dry (highs 85-95°F), low humidity
  • Fall: Spectacular mountain colors
  • Winter: Cold with moderate snow (less than the mountains)
  • Spring: Mild with occasional late-season snow

The foothills position means Herriman often escapes the valley inversions that can affect lower-elevation areas.

Making the Move

  1. Visit: Schedule a trip to explore neighborhoods and get a feel for the community

  3. Explore Communities: Tour developments like Panorama to see available homes
  4. Connect with Locals: Facebook groups and community forums offer insider insights

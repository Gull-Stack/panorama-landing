The Complete Guide to Herriman Utah Schools

9 min read • Updated February 2026

For families considering a move to Herriman, schools are often the top priority. Good news: Jordan School District serves Herriman with some of Utah's highest-rated schools at every level. Here's everything parents need to know.

Jordan School District Overview

Jordan School District is one of Utah's largest and most respected districts, serving approximately 58,000 students across southwest Salt Lake County. The district consistently outperforms state averages in standardized testing and graduation rates.

Key district facts:

  • 56 schools serving Herriman and surrounding cities
  • 90%+ graduation rate
  • Strong STEM and career technical education programs
  • Robust special education and gifted services
  • Active parent involvement culture

High Schools Serving Herriman

Herriman High School

Grades 10-12 • Opened 2012 • ~2,400 students

A Rated

Herriman High offers a comprehensive academic program with strong AP course offerings, competitive athletics (state championships in multiple sports), and award-winning performing arts. The school features modern facilities including a state-of-the-art auditorium and athletic complex.

Mountain Ridge High School

Grades 10-12 • Opened 2018 • ~2,000 students

A Rated

Mountain Ridge is one of Utah's newest high schools, featuring cutting-edge technology throughout. The school has quickly established itself with strong academics and athletics, and offers unique programs in biotechnology and engineering.

Middle Schools

Fort Herriman Middle School

Grades 7-9 • ~1,200 students

A- Rated

Copper Mountain Middle School

Grades 7-9 • ~1,100 students

A Rated

Both middle schools offer comprehensive programs including honors tracks, orchestra, band, choir, and competitive sports. The junior high model (grades 7-9) provides an extra year of preparation before high school.

Special Programs

Jordan School District offers numerous specialized programs:

  • Gifted and Talented: GATE programs at elementary level, honors/AP tracks in secondary
  • Dual Immersion: Spanish, French, and Chinese language programs
  • Career Technical Education: Healthcare, IT, manufacturing pathways
  • Special Education: Comprehensive services for diverse learning needs
  • Online Learning: Jordan Virtual Learning Academy options

Private School Options

While most Herriman families choose public schools, private options within a reasonable drive include:

  • American Preparatory Academy (charter, multiple locations)
  • Waterford School (independent, Sandy)
  • Various religious-affiliated schools in the Salt Lake Valley

Tips for Families

  • Tour Schools: Jordan School District welcomes prospective family visits
  • Check Boundaries: Use the district's boundary lookup tool for your specific address
  • Review Calendars: Note that Utah uses a different schedule than some states
  • Connect Early: Join school Facebook groups to connect with other parents
